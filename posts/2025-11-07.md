California:
Voters overwhelming voted for prop 50, the redistricting reform meant to counter Trump's 
power grab in Texas. It is currently winning by a margin of 28 points.
That margin is greater than the margin Kamala had in the presidential election,
and greater than the margins Gavin Newsom got in any of his gubernatorial elections.
Results also showed eight Californian counties that Trump won in 2024 but supported 
Proposition 50 this past Tuesday: Fresno (where Trump beat Harris by a 4.38% margin), 
Merced (4.36%), Inyo (2.98%), San Bernardino (2.14%), Lake (1.37%), Riverside (1.26%), 
San Joaquin (0.89%), and Imperial (0.86%).

Virginia: 
Abigail Spanberger won in Virginia by roughly 15 points.
That's a 17 point swing from 4 years ago where Rs won by 2.
Democrats entered the night with a 51 to 49 majority in the House of Delegates.
Not only did they retain their majority, they gained 13+ seats (there is one race still 
too close to call), expanding their House of Delegates majority to at least 64 seats, 
the biggest majority since 1987. Keeping the majority also means they can push forward 
with their plans to follow California in redrawing their maps.

New Jersey:
Mikie Sherrill won in New Jersey by about 13 points.
This is about a 10 point swing from 4 years ago.
Polls shows a close race but Sherril outperformed her polls by 8 to 10 points 
Democrats also flipped 3 Assembly seats growing their 
Assembly majority from 52 to 55 seats (out of 80), 
their largest in 52 years, securing a supermajority.

Georgia: 
Democrats flipped 2 statewide Public Service Commission seats. 
Peter Hubbard and Alicia Johnson both won with about 60 percent of the vote.
First time since 2006 that Democrats have won a non-federal statewide office in Georgia.

Pennsylvania:
Voters kept 3 Democratic Pennsylvania Supreme Court justices, defeating a multimillion-dollar 
conservative campaign run by billionaire Jeff Yass who was compared to Elon Musk.
Democrats also won 2 statewide judicial special elections for the Pennsylvania 
Superior Court and Commonwealth Court. In Bucks County, Democrats swept all top county offices, 
electing the county‚Äôs first-ever Democratic district attorney and defeating the GOP sheriff.
Democrats also won big in Erie, Lehigh, and Northampton counties ‚Äî all presidential bellwethers.

Mississippi:
Democrats broke the GOP supermajority in the state Senate.
Flipped 2 Senate seats and 1 House seat after court-ordered 
redistricting expanded Black representation.

Texas:
In a district Trump won by 2024, Democrat Taylor Rehmet will be heading to a runoff election 
for the Texas SD-9 state senate seat. He faced off against "UltraMAGA" Republican 
Leigh Wambsganss, and a more run of the mill Republican John Huffman. 
No one won a majority, but Rehmet won a plurality 47.6% of the vote. He will be 
facing Wambsganss who got 36% of the vote in a runoff election.
